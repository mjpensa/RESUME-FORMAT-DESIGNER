## Eye-Tracking & Recruiter Behavior

- Recruiters spend 7.4 seconds on initial resume scan — TheLadders 2018 Eye-Tracking Study
- F-pattern scanning: horizontal across top, then down left margin, then across again — Nielsen Norman Group
- Six fixation points: name, current title, current company, dates, previous title, education — TheLadders 2012
- 70% of initial scan time on top third of page — University of Michigan 2023, MDPI (https://www.mdpi.com/2504-4990/5/3/38)
- Professionally re-written resumes scored 6.2/7 vs. 3.9/7 for originals — TheLadders 2018
- ML classifier predicts resume approval with AUC 0.767 from eye-tracking data — MDPI 2023
- Dense text blocks get skipped; scannable content with numbers gets more dwell time — multiple

## ATS & Keyword Data

- 99.7% of recruiters use keyword filters in ATS — Jobscan State of Job Search 2025
- 97.8% of Fortune 500 use ATS — Jobscan 2025
- Workday is most widely used ATS among Fortune 500 — Jobscan 2025
- Modern ATS uses AI context understanding; keyword stuffing is detectable — multiple (needs verification)
- .docx is safest format for ATS — multiple (needs verification)

## Bullet Point Best Practices

- Formula: [Action Verb] + [Context] + [Quantified Result] — Columbia, Yale career services
- 80% of bullets should contain quantifiable results — Wonsulting 2025
- 95% of content should be accomplishments, not responsibilities — Jane Heifetz via HBR
- Consulting bullets should demonstrate problem-solving, personal impact, entrepreneurial drive — MBB guides

## Tone & AI Detection

- 74% of hiring managers have detected AI-generated content — Jobscan 2025
- 58% of hiring managers concerned about AI-generated applications — Jobscan 2025
- AI tells: overly smooth language, generic phrasing, lack of specific detail — needs verification
- Best tone: active, specific, concrete, quantified

## Structure & Summary

- Professional summary: 3-4 sentences, paragraph form — HBS Alumni career services
- First 15-20 words are critical attention window — John Lees via HBR
- Lead with descriptor matching target role — HBR
- 2 pages acceptable for senior professionals — HBR, multiple
